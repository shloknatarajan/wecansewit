Tutorials

N95 Mask Covers

Did you know that doctors in the U.S. are being told to use the same N95 mask for a week at a time in order to make supplies last? Washable cloth mask covers allows users to keep using their N95 mask while washing the outside cover, increasing safety and hygeine. Read instructions for sewing mask covers here.

Cloth Masks

The CDC has just recommended that all citizens wear masks especially in areas of signficant community based transmission. While most hospitals currently prefer N95 mask covers, many are also accepting cloth masks. There is also demand from local homeless shelters and nursing homes, check the linked document in the Facilities in Need tab for more information. Written sewing instructions can be found here. If you'd prefer a video, we found an excellent one here.

Isolation Gowns

Isolation gowns are critical in keeping germs and bodily fluids from coming in contact with skin or clothing. See instructions for making isolation gowns here.

3D Printed Face Shields

While traditional cloth masks on their own have shortcomings in protecting from the spread of the virus, cloth masks when combined with face shields have been shown to be considerable more effective in stopping the spread of pathogens. Constructing a face shield requires the use of a 3D printer. Instructions and print files can be found here.
